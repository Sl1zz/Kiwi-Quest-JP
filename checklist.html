Guides and info

Top 10 things to do from the itinerary

  • Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa, Tokyo

    Senso-ji is Tokyo's oldest temple and one of its most significant. The Kaminarimon Gate, adorned with a massive red lantern, serves as the entrance to Nakamise Shopping Street. This bustling street offers a wide array of traditional Japanese goods and street food. Beyond the gate, the temple itself is a place of spiritual reverence and beauty, making it a must-visit in Tokyo.

  • Fushimi Inari Taisha in Kyoto

    Fushimi Inari Taisha is renowned for its thousands of vivid orange torii gates that create a stunning tunnel leading to the sacred Mount Inari. This place holds immense spiritual significance for the Japanese people, and it's a picturesque spot for visitors. Don't miss the opportunity to hike to the summit for breathtaking panoramic views of Kyoto and the surrounding landscapes.

  • Miyajima Island near Hiroshima

    Miyajima Island is known for its iconic Itsukushima Shrine, with a floating torii gate that appears to rest on the water during high tide. The island itself offers enchanting walking trails, friendly deer that roam freely, and delectable grilled oysters – a local specialty. It's a serene destination where nature and culture harmoniously coexist.

  • Osaka Castle in Osaka

    Osaka Castle is not just a historical site; it's a symbol of the city's rich heritage. The castle is surrounded by a beautifully landscaped park, offering visitors a glimpse into Japan's history and architectural prowess. Inside the castle, a museum displays artifacts and information about its intriguing past. The surrounding park is an ideal place for picnics and leisurely strolls.

  • Himeji Castle in Himeji

    Himeji Castle, often referred to as the White Heron Castle due to its striking white facade, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Japan's most iconic castles. The castle's intricate wooden structure and beautiful interior are a testament to Japanese architecture and design. Climbing to the top provides a fantastic vantage point to appreciate the city's layout and history.

  • Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) in Kyoto

    Kinkaku-ji, the Golden Pavilion, is a Zen Buddhist temple covered in gold leaf. Its reflection in the Mirror Pond creates a breathtaking sight, making it one of Kyoto's most celebrated landmarks. The surrounding gardens enhance the temple's tranquil ambiance, making it an ideal spot for reflection and photography.

  • Nara Park in Nara

    Nara Park is a unique destination famous for the hundreds of sacred deer that roam freely throughout the area. These deer are protected and cherished in the city. While visiting, you can feed and interact with these friendly animals. Additionally, Nara Park is home to significant temples like Todai-ji, which houses the giant Great Buddha (Daibutsu).

  • Toshogu Shrine in Nikko

    Toshogu Shrine in Nikko is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its intricate carvings, lush natural surroundings, and historical significance. The shrine's architectural details and serene atmosphere make it a cultural gem. Located within Nikko National Park, it offers a harmonious blend of culture and nature.

  • Kanazawa's Kenrokuen Garden

    Kenrokuen Garden in Kanazawa is regarded as one of Japan's most exquisite traditional gardens. Its seasonal beauty captivates visitors throughout the year. The garden exemplifies classic Japanese landscaping principles, offering a tranquil and harmonious environment. Visiting Kenrokuen is an opportunity to immerse yourself in Japanese garden design and aesthetics.

  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove in Kyoto

    Arashiyama Bamboo Grove is a mesmerizing bamboo forest located in the district of Arashiyama. This enchanting natural wonder is particularly captivating during the early morning and late afternoon, when the sunlight filters through the towering bamboo shoots. Nearby, you'll find the Monkey Park where you can observe Japanese macaques, adding an extra layer of charm to the experience.
