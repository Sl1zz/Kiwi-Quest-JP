FAQ

► Is Japan a safe destination for New Zealand travelers?

Japan is generally considered a safe destination. The crime rate is low, and locals are very friendly and welcoming to tourists. However, like any other place, it's essential to stay vigilant and take common-sense precautions.

► Do I need a visa to visit Japan as a New Zealander?

New Zealand citizens don't need a visa to visit Japan for up to 90 days as tourists. You'll receive a temporary visitor visa upon arrival, provided you don't plan to work in Japan. Just ensure your passport remains valid throughout your stay.

► What's the best time to visit Japan from New Zealand?

The best time to visit Japan from New Zealand is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). These seasons offer pleasant weather and beautiful scenery.

► What's the currency in Japan, and should I exchange currency before my trip?

The currency in Japan is the Japanese Yen (JPY). It's recommended to exchange currency at the airport or local banks for competitive rates.

► Do I need to learn Japanese before visiting Japan?

While learning some basic Japanese phrases can be helpful and appreciated, many signs and menus in popular tourist areas are available in English. ► Can I use my New Zealand electrical devices in Japan?

Japan uses a different type of power plug (Type A) than New Zealand (Type I). You'll need a plug adapter to use your New Zealand devices in Japan. The voltage is 100V in Japan, so ensure your devices are compatible.

► What's the best way to get around Japan as a New Zealander tourist?

Japan has an excellent and efficient public transportation system, including trains, buses, and subways. Consider purchasing a Japan Rail Pass if you plan to travel between cities. Local transportation within cities is also easy to use.

► Is Wi-Fi readily available in Japan for New Zealand travelers?

Wi-Fi is widely available in Japan, including at airports, hotels, and many public places. You can also rent portable Wi-Fi devices, which are convenient for staying connected while traveling.
